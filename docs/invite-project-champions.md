We're hosting the first **Gies AI For Impact Challenge** — a 24-hour event where 80-120 Gies students will build AI agents that automate common business workflows. We'd love your involvement, and there are several ways to participate — from hands-on to hands-off.

## Start Here: Orientation on April 10 (No Commitment)

Before you decide anything, join us for an online orientation on **Friday, April 10 from 2:00 - 3:00 PM on MS Teams** with **Jake Myers**, Lead for Enterprise GenAI Solutions working with the Office of the CIO at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.

**This session is open to anyone curious about automation — no commitment to the challenge required.**

## Ways to Participate

There's no single way to be involved. Pick the role that fits your interest and availability:

**Option A: Propose a Challenge**
Suggest a business process that would benefit from automation. This doesn't need to be specific to your actual systems — challenges can be framed generically around common business scenarios (e.g., "streamline an employee onboarding workflow" rather than "fix our specific HR system"). Think of it as describing the *type* of work, not your internal tools. Students benefit from the realism, and you get to see how automation could apply to work like yours.

**Option B: Provide Business Context as a Mentor**
Don't have a specific challenge in mind? You can still participate by being available to student teams as a **domain mentor** — someone who understands how your functional area actually works. Students building a finance automation need to talk to someone in finance. Your real-world perspective is valuable even without a formal challenge.

**Option C: Both**
Propose a challenge *and* be available as a mentor to the teams working on it. This is the most engaged role, and champions who take it on tend to get the most out of the event.

We have five tracks:

| Track | Example Challenges |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Finance & Accounting** | Invoice processing, expense management, financial reporting workflows |
| **HR** | Candidate screening, onboarding processes, employee inquiry routing |
| **Marketing & Sales** | Content generation, campaign management, analytics automation |
| **Business Technology** | System integrations, IT service automation, data pipeline workflows |
| **General Innovation** | Any operational process you wish ran itself |

## About the Data: Nothing Real, Nothing Live

We want to be clear: **no real Gies data, systems, or integrations are involved.** All student projects will use simulated or synthetic data. No one will be connecting to your actual tools or testing automations on live systems. The challenges are framed as realistic business scenarios — the kind of work that happens across any organization — not as projects tied to specific internal processes.

This is a learning exercise for students and a window into what's possible for staff. There is zero operational risk.

## What You Get Out of It

- **See automation in action.** Students will build solutions using tools like Microsoft Copilot Studio. Watching your challenge get tackled gives you concrete insights into what's possible — and what you might want to explore in your own work.
- **Build your own intuition for AI tools.** Even without touching a system, you'll walk away understanding what Copilot Studio and similar platforms can do, and where they hit limits.
- **Support student career development.** Students gain professional experience working on realistic business problems. Your participation gives their portfolio projects the credibility that comes from real business context.
- **Low effort, high visibility.** Connect with students, faculty, and leadership in a setting that showcases Gies innovation.
