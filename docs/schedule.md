## Executive Summary

We propose a 24-hour event focused on empowering Gies College of Business students to build AI agent solutions that automate real business workflows. This initiative aims to democratize AI development, enabling students across all business disciplines to create practical solutions.

## Event Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 24 hours |
| **Proposed Date/Time** | 5 pm Thursday April 23rd — 5 pm Friday April 24th |
| **Target Audience** | Gies College of Business undergraduate and graduate students |
| **Format** | In person with in-person/online workshops in preceding weeks |
| **Expected Attendance** | 80-120 students (organized in teams of 2-4), 8-10 Gies staff as champions, 5-8 technical mentors |

**Rules:**
- Explain what process automation is, and the value it can bring to Gies and student's professional development
- No chatbots or simple solutions to automation

**Focus Areas:**
- Finance automation (invoice processing, expense management, financial reporting)
- HR workflows (candidate screening, onboarding, employee inquiries)
- Marketing operations (content generation, campaign management, analytics)
- Operations & supply chain (inventory management, vendor communications)
- Customer Operations (ticket routing, response automation)

## Alignment with Gies AI Guidelines

This event embodies the Gies AI Guidelines that guide responsible AI engagement across our community:

- **Human Leadership and Purposeful Innovation**: Students lead the creative process, using AI as a tool to augment their business judgment and domain expertise.
- **AI as a Partner in Perpetual Learning**: The workshop series and hands-on building cultivate curiosity and higher-order thinking through experimentation.
- **Integrity and Transparency**: Teams document their development process and clearly communicate how AI contributes to their solutions.
- **Sustainable and Meaningful AI Practice**: Projects focus on creating genuine business value while encouraging thoughtful, appropriate use of AI.
- **Inclusive Access and Belonging**: The no-code focus ensures students across all business disciplines can participate as builders.

## Responsible AI & Data Handling (Risk Controls)

To ensure safe and compliant participation:

- **No PII or confidential data.** Only synthetic, de-identified, or pre-approved sample datasets may be used.
- **Human-in-the-loop required** for any outward-facing communications (emails, customer responses, vendor messages).
- Teams must disclose: tools used, model/provider, and where data was processed/stored.
- Clear code of conduct, escalation path, and event supervision plan for oversight.
